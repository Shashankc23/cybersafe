🌐 Safe Browsing

Stay Sharp Online!

Learn three simple habits that can help you browse the internet safely and avoid common online traps.

🔍 Thing 1: Always Check the Address Bar

Scammers build websites that look almost identical to real ones, using the same logos, colours, and layouts.

The address bar is often the easiest way to spot the fake.

💡 Remember
Real: bbc.com or bbc.co.uk
Fake: bbc-news-today.info

Always make sure the website address matches the company you're trying to visit.

🔒 Thing 2: HTTPS and the Padlock Matter

Safe websites usually begin with https:// and display a padlock icon in the address bar.

This means your information is encrypted while it's being sent over the internet.

💡 Remember
Look for https:// 🔒 before entering passwords or personal information. If a site only uses http://, be extra careful.

🚨 Thing 3: Watch Out for Downloads and Fake Pop-ups

Some websites try to scare or trick you into downloading harmful software.

  • A file starts downloading without you clicking anything.
  • A pop-up says "Virus Detected! Call this number now!"
⚠️ Watch Out
Close the tab, don't open unexpected downloads, and tell a trusted adult if something seems suspicious.
