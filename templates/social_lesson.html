🎭 Social Engineering

Don't Be Manipulated!

Learn how scammers use psychology—not hacking—to trick people into giving away information.

👮 Thing 1: Beware of Fake Authority Figures

Scammers often pretend to work for a game company, your school, or another trusted organization to make you feel like you have to follow their instructions.

They might say, "I work for Roblox. Your account will be deleted unless you give me your password."

⚠️ Watch Out
Real companies will never ask for your password through chat messages or direct messages.

😤 Thing 2: Don't Give In to Pressure

Manipulators try to make you act before you have time to think. They may pressure, threaten, or dare you into doing something.

  • Threaten to embarrass you.
  • Dare you to "prove" yourself.
  • Create fake urgency so you panic.
💡 Remember
If someone is rushing or pressuring you, slow down and think before you respond.

🤝 Thing 3: Don't Trust Someone Just Because They Know a Name

Scammers sometimes mention a friend, family member, or something personal about you to make it seem like they're trustworthy.

For example: "Your best friend told me your birthday, I just forgot it. Can you remind me?"

💡 Remember
If someone you don't recognize mentions a friend's name, check with that friend directly before sharing any information.
